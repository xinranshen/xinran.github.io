Baudrillard points out that the progress in technology and media has eliminated our private universes and made explicit every object. These made us live in a spectacle society in which both our information sovereignty and independent thinking is harmed.
2
Marx, however, argues that such explicitness and straight-forwardness, or, obscenity, as phrased by Baudrillard, of objects, is a key characteristic of consumer society, in which everything has their equivalence in the circulation system. So it seems that this obscenity is inevitable.
3
All these thoughts remind me of Susan Sontag who believes that we should return things to their original appearance and not add shallow, subjective and stigmatized interpretations of them. In today's society, because the presentation of things has become direct or even superficial, people are used to subjective judgment rather than rational thinking.
